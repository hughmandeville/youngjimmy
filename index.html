Christopher 'YOUNG JIMMY' Beckwith is a 33-year-old Guitarist, Rapper, Singer-Song Writer, Producer, Graphic Designer, and Video Editor based in Brooklyn, NY. Raised in The Bronx, East Chester Garden's located in the poorest, Congressional District 16, is Notoriously known for the largest gang takedown in New York City history. Young Jimmy picked up the guitar at age 6. A high school dropout, he attended the Art & Design High School class of 2006 and attended Greenwich House Music School. Studied Classical, Jazz, and Rock music performed monthly recitals under a scholarship.

At age 19, he moved to the original House of Yes. Working as a Lead Guitarist for various Rock and Hip Hop acts in the New York City area. House of Yes would later burn down in a fire with half of his musical equipment caused by ex-girlfriend negligence. Poverty and homelessness made life a constant struggle. In unfortunate times he slept on trains and park benches. Coming of age, influenced by 2pac, Andre 3000, Thin Lizzy, RHCP, The Police, Micheal Jackson, Prince, STP, Raekwon, Nirvana, Run-D.M.C, Sublime, MF Doom, Rage Against the Machine, Jimmy Page, and Jimi Hendrix.

In 2009 Young Jimmy recorded an EP called 'To My Chagrin' with an all-black indie-rock band 'Sankofa' with producer-engineer Paul Mahajan. 'Sankofa' name comes from Ghana means "go back and get it" we must look back to our past so that we may understand how we became what we are and move forward to a better future. Cocaine, ego, and women ended the band 3-year run.

Growing up in The Bronx, rock music was not the norm by no means the birthplace of Hip Hop. Young Jimmy was the juxtapose to an environment dominated by Hip Hop, gangs, guns, violence, and Building fires. Raised by a single mother in public housing with three siblings, his stepfather was Hip Hop Legend, and Rock & Roll Hall of Famer Grand Master Mele Mel helped nurture his musical ambitions. Introduced Young Jimmy to the music industry and accompanied Mele Mel on a few sold-out shows playing guitar. Young Jimmy describes his style of music as Hood Rock, a Rap-Rock alternative rock subgenre.

Young Jimmy has shared the stage and opened for: Kendrick Lamar, Big Krit, Freddie Gibbs, Saul Williams, Talib Kweli & Michelle Williams, Smoke Dza, Res, Ces Cru, Tanya Morgan, Lil Debbie, Jah C & The Antidote, Jay Boogie, Caviliar, Doodlebug of Digable Planets, and many others.

Afro-Punk documentary by filmmaker James Spooner best describes the 'Black Rocker' experience. Black people created rock music in the early 1950s. For the last 50 years, the genre has been dominated and appropriated by white people mainly, white men. Here are excerpts from Jack Hamilton's book 'Just Around Midnight' speaks to the plight of the 'Black Rocker'-

"Being a white man had become preconditioned for making "real" rock music."

"No black derived musical form in American history has more assiduously moved to erase and blockade participation than rock music."

"Which brings us to the third, and by far most common, way that the "whitening" of rock and roll music has been discussed, Simply not at all."

Young Jimmy's debut studio album sets the record straight, due to be released in, 2021 allows listeners to dive into his 'raw' unfiltered "HOOD ROCK" fusion sound.
